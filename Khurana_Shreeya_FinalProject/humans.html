The Effect on Humans

Many people find that every night, there is some sort of light streaming through their windows, disturbing their sleep.
Not only does it disturb sleep but this light may also play a role in developing illnesses. Light is considered as an interfering
and annoying factor that suppresses natural melatonin levels in humans at night. Melatonin is a hormone that is produced exclusively
at night in the dark and is an important output of the body's inherent 24-hour clock (or circadian rhythm). Many biological processes are
regulated by the circadian rhythm, including the sleep-wake cycle and regulating a range of hormones that influence certain cancers, such as breast and prostate cancers
